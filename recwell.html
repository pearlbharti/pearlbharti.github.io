PROBLEM

Disjointed Navigation and Lack of Centralized Information

The current RecWell website struggles to effectively deliver critical information and resources to users. Key features, such as machine availability, live facility usage, and detailed facility tours, are either scattered or absent, leading to confusion and increased effort for users seeking relevant details. Additionally, the homepage lacks optimization for prioritizing essential services, requiring excessive scrolling and making navigation unintuitive. This disjointed information architecture impacts user experience and limits engagement with the site’s offerings.

SOLUTION

Refining Information Architecture and Expanding Critical Key Pages


SOLUTION #1

Crucial and Optimized Information Display

The redesigned homepage includes essential information prominently to enhance user access.The homepage minimizes scrolling and maximizes space with quick links, aligning with user and business goals.


SOLUTION #2

Comprehensive Facilities and Tours Page

A centralized page serves as a one-stop shop for all facilities and tours offered by RecWell, streamlining access to information that is currently scattered across the site.


SOLUTION #3

Prioritized Key Services

Key features such as live usage stats, upcoming events, available programs, and daily schedules are strategically showcased for easy visibility.s

RESEARCH

Competitor Analysis


ANALYSIS #1

University of Texas, Austin

Known for its large student body and diverse recreational programs, UT Austin’s website offers insights into how a major university manages information-heavy services effectively.


Pros:

  • Clear navigation with well-organized categories for fitness, facilities, and memberships.
  • Interactive tools such as live facility usage and class schedules.
  • Vibrant visuals and engaging design that reflect the brand identity.

Cons:

  • Overwhelming amount of information on the homepage, potentially leading to cognitive overload.
  • Some pages require multiple clicks to access critical information, adding friction to the user journey.
ANALYSIS #2

Massachusetts Institute of Technology

With its reputation for innovation and user-centric design, MIT’s recreation website serves as a model for integrating technology into wellness services.


Pros:

  • Minimalistic and clean design, emphasizing functionality and ease of use.
  • Centralized location for all essential services, such as facility hours, programs, and memberships.
  • Seamless integration of interactive features like event registration and real-time updates.

Cons:

  • Lack of visual appeal; the design may feel too sterile for a wellness-focused website.
  • Limited focus on promoting community events or fostering engagement beyond functional needs.
Analysis #3

University of British Columbia

Key features such as live usage stats, upcoming events, available programs, and daily schedules are strategically showcased for easy visibility.s


Pros:

  • Real-time facility updates help users plan visits effectively.
  • Simplified, intuitive navigation makes finding resources effortless.
  • Dynamic visuals and event highlights enhance homepage engagement.

Cons:

RESEARCH

User Interviews

User interviews were conducted to gather in-depth insights into the challenges and needs of RecWell website users, which quantitative data alone couldn’t reveal. The questions focused on navigation issues, feature gaps, and user expectations, ensuring actionable feedback to guide the design of a more intuitive and user-centered platform.

  1. How do you usually find information about RecWell's facilities, events, or programs on the website?
  2. What challenges do you encounter when navigating the RecWell website to find specific details or plan your visit?
  3. Can you describe an ideal experience when accessing information about RecWell’s services or facilities?
  4. What types of information or features do you feel are missing or difficult to find on the RecWell website?
  5. How do you currently use the RecWell website to plan your fitness activities or events?
  6. Are there any other websites or platforms you use for recreation or wellness, and what features from those would you want to see on RecWell’s site?
  7. If you could change one thing about the RecWell website to make it more useful or user-friendly, what would it be?
RESEARCH

What I concluded...

From 10 user interviews, I learned that RecWell users appreciate the variety of programs and facilities offered. However, many expressed frustration with the difficulty of navigating the website and scheduling classes. Four interviewees mentioned that they often avoid using the site altogether due to this inconvenience.



User reviews on our posts in subreddits along with the user interviews backed up with our data analysis led to some great insights.

Redesign Goals

I conducted interviews and sent out a survey, sampling the viewpoints users had towards wellbeing and Rec Well's services, how and why they made decisions about the usage of services.
To enhance student well-being and satisfaction, the Rec Well platform redesign focused on simplifying access to services, improving navigation, and addressing usability issues. The goal was to provide a user-centric experience that empowers students/ faculty or the community members to easily explore and benefit from RecWell's offerings.



Enable students to view real-time building usage and available equipments with minimal friction.

Implement safeguards and clear error messaging to prevent accidental data loss during form submissions or bookings.

Simplify the platform's structure to help users quickly locate programs, schedules, and facility information

Ensure the platform is mobile-friendly and meets accessibility standards for diverse user needs.

Reflection

Future Work

As my research and design process highlighted critical areas for improvement on the RecWell platform, I envision several next steps to refine and elevate the user experience as we intend to implement the design changes in the next FY-

  • Comprehensive Usability Testing - Conduct in-depth usability tests with active RecWell users to observe their interactions with the platform, identify pain points, and gather feedback for iterative design improvements.
  • Accessibility Enhancements - Prioritize integrating accessibility features, such as screen reader compatibility, improved keyboard navigation, and proper color contrast, to ensure the platform is inclusive for all users.
  • Refinement of Prototypes - Expand on the lo-fi prototype with additional time and resources, creating higher fidelity designs that better capture the nuances of user needs and expectations.
