01 Background

I started my journey with a B.E in computer engineering, building AI systems, coding frontend platforms, and debugging backend workflows. I loved solving problems at the system level: untangling logic, scaling structure, and making things work. But I kept seeing the same gap- products that functioned technically, but fell apart at the human layer.

That’s what pulled me into product design. I wanted to shape not just how things work, but how they feel, how people understand, trust, and use them. Today, I still think like an engineer- but I design like someone obsessed with turning systems into experiences.

02 Now

Right now, I’m pursuing my Master’s in Information Science (UX Design/Research) at UW–Madison. I’ve worked on logistics platforms, and data-heavy systems across B2B and B2C SaaS. My work blends deep research, structure, and systems thinking—focused on clarity, not just polish.

Beyond product design, As a Teaching Assistant I’ve mentored over 200 students in UX, web development, accessibility, and research-led design. I also currently lead as the secretary of the official UX Club of UW-Madison, a growing design community where I’ve hosted workshops, led critiques, and organized talks with teams from Amazon, Epic, and Lenovo. It’s a space to mentor, learn, and push the field forward together.

Snippets of my life outside of design

When I’m not designing, I’m either painting, playing guitar, experimenting in the kitchen, or accidentally losing four hours watching Sisyphy55 or Kurzgesagt in a Nutshell. I love systems, whether they’re in Figma, the universe, or a very stubborn Kerala chicken curry recipe.
